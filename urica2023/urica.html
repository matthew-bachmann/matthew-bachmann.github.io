A CONFERENCE FOR UPCOMING RESEARCHERS IN COMMUTATIVE ALGEBRA

After a two year hiatus, the twentieth annual URICA (formerly KUMUNUjr) conference will be on May 6-7, at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln.

Inspired by the success of the KUMUNU commutative algebra conferences (KU, MU, NU), KUMUNUjr brought together graduate students and postdocs studying commutative algebra in the midwest to share their research and expertise while building lasting collaborations and friendships. Upcoming Researchers in Commutative Algebra, URiCA, is the next chapter for KUMUNUjr. We want to continue our mission to promote interaction among junior researchers, graduate students and postdocs, while branching outside our initial triad of universities.

This year's conference will host a number of 40 minute and 20 minute talks as well as a panel of recent commutative algebra PhDs to talk about their career paths. Note that UNL is also hosting the commutative algebra conference KUMUNU on May 7-8, immediately following URiCA. If you plan to attend both conferences, you need only register with us and indicate that you want to attend both conferences on your registration form.

Registration information

There is a block of hotel rooms reserved for those who register by April 10th. We will be able to provide mileage reimbursement for those traveling by car. For support with airfare, please email one of the organizers. We may still be able to reimburse some lodging or travel if you need to register after the deadline.

If you plan to attened both URiCA and KUMUNU (occuring the same weekend), you should inidicate so on your registration form with us. You will not need to fill out a second registration form with KUMUNU.
