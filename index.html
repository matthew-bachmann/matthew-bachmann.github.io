I am an Adjunct Lecturer in Mathematics at Davidson College. My research interests are in applications of homological algebra and derived category methods to commutative algebra. I received my PhD in Mathematics from the University of Nebraska-Lincoln in August 2023 advised by Mark Walker.

Besides pure math research, I have many other professional interests. I am currently iterested in projects with a data science flavor. This year I am teaching a statistics course with emphasis on applications in R and data visualization. I also enjoy picking up basic html/css skills in order to design my website (which is why it is quite plain but getting better!). For fun I enjoy movies, playing chess, reading (contemporary fiction and fantasy), and trying new restaurants. If you're interested in any of these things and want to chat, please reach out!
