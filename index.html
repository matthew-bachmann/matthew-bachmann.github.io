I am a mathematics graduate student at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln and am expected to earn my PhD in May 2024. My research interests are in applications of homological algebra and derived category methods to commutative algebra. My research advisor is Mark Walker.

Besides pure math research, I have many other professional interests. I am passionate about mathematical mentorship and outreach. Currently, I am part of the organizing committee for the Upcoming Researchers in Commutative Algebra (URiCA) conference. I also helped found the Directed Reading Program (DRP) at UNL with my friend Austin Eide. Additionally, I like to discuss mathematics education and am always striving to be a better teacher (in the classroom and out). I'm also eager to learn more about html/css and programming. Though they aren't much, I've had a lot of fun learning about how to design my website and the websites listed in the top right. If you're interested in any of these things and want to chat, please reach out!
